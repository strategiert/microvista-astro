## What is an X-ray Detector?

An X-ray detector is the image sensor that converts transmitted X-ray radiation into digital signals. Its hardware characteristics determine what information quality is later available in the volume dataset.

## Scientific background

Technically relevant are the scintillator, pixel matrix, DQE and MTF. Throughout the complete measurement chain these factors influence signal quality, artifact susceptibility and metrological stability.

## Relevant key metrics

- Stability of signal formation across time and load states.
- Contribution to effective resolution and contrast level.
- Robustness against drift, defective pixels and thermal influences.

## Standards and thresholds

- **Standards:** ISO 17636-2:2022 (corr. 2023-02) as well as ISO 15708-2:2025 (equipment/samples/principles).
- **Typical thresholds (in practice):** Defective pixels are compensated by a correction map; remaining uncompensated pixels in the ROI typically near 0 %.
- **Validity:** Detector limits are defined by the manufacturer datasheet, calibration and inspection-task acceptance.

## Application in industrial practice

- Adaptation of the measurement chain to dense or multi-material parts.
- Reliable defect detection on small structures.
- Reproducible series inspection with consistent quality metrics.
